Waste not want not - Re-purposing smartphones that are being upgraded for newer models

One problem facing the tech industry today is the mountain of smart devices that are being discarded each year just because there is a newer, shinier model out that the manufacturer has to sell.


The problem is that these smart devices are probably mostly fully functional still. This problem is probabaly not restricted to smart devices, but electronic devices and appliances in general.


The difference with the smart devices that are being discarded is that they are perfect for re-purposing to fulfil their true destiny as the very small re-programmable computing devices they were designed to be.


Not only do these smartphones provide a platform for running custom applications, but most of them will have camera's, WiFi, Bluetooth, touch screen's, microphones, speakers, gyro's and accelerometers, and many will have GPS as well.


Basically, the smartphones can hear, talk, see, know whether they are in motion, know where they are, and feel....ok this last point might be a bit optimistic!, but thats a lot of technology, just being binned.


These devices could be used a application platform's that can make use of their special hardware and small size and robustness, no moving parts makes them ideal for harsh environments or environments where mechanical shock is a possibility.


This re-purposing would serve 2 ends;

  • Saving more electronic devices just being dumped
    • Providing low-cost alternatives for some of the devices they are being re-purposed for


Some possible applications for re-purposed smartphones.

  • Home security. camera. phone. internet access, accelerometer
  • Remote controls. TV's stereos, garages. Bluetooth, Wifi
  • Door-bells, with video built in, touch-screen for button
  • Home automation controllers
  • Intercom systems, via WiFi
  • Weather stations, with appropriate sensors attached
  • Earthquake sensors, with accelerometer, GPS
  • House flooding sensors, with SMS notifications


About the author : My early background is in electronics design and I have a passion in automation, home and commercial, as well as robotics and general tinkering, this sort of re-purposing really interest's me. I have had experience writing applications for mobile devices and accessing the various sensors available with smartphones is surprisingly not that difficult.
